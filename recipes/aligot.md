# Aligot

> Decadent French cheesy mashed potatoes — riced Yukon Golds beaten with Gruyère and Port Salut, cream and room-temperature butter into a glossy, stretchy, silk-like ribbon. Chef Jean-Pierre style.

**Servings:** ~6 as a side

## Ingredients

- 3–3.5 lb Yukon Gold potatoes (cooked whole, skin on)
- A few cloves garlic, finely sliced
- 8 oz high-melting cheese, shredded — a mix of Gruyère and Port Salut (traditional is Tome Fraîche; Port Salut or Fontina substitute well and melt without getting gummy)
- 6 oz unsalted butter, cut into small pieces, at room temperature (not cold, not melted/clarified)
- ½–¾ cup heavy whipping cream
- Salt and pepper, to taste (white pepper is traditional for a clean colour)

## Method

> The trick is a smooth, silk-like ribbon texture without the starch turning gummy. Use a **potato ricer** — never an electric mixer or food processor.

1. **Boil:** Place the whole, unpeeled potatoes in a pot of cold water with the sliced garlic. Bring to a boil and cook ~25–30 min, until a toothpick slides easily through the centre.
2. **Peel & rice:** Drain. While still very hot, peel off the skins. Pass the hot potatoes and the poached garlic together through a potato ricer directly into a clean pot set over very low heat.
3. **Cheese:** Keeping the pot over low heat, fold in all 8 oz of the shredded Gruyère and Port Salut.
4. **Cream & season:** Pour in an initial ½ cup of cream, then salt and pepper. Stir vigorously by hand with a sturdy spoon until the cheese melts completely into the potatoes.
5. **Beat in butter:** Add the room-temperature butter a little at a time, working by hand until it emulsifies. You want a glossy, smooth, elastic texture that stretches when lifted.

> Emulsion note: don't use cold butter (it drops the temperature too fast) or melted butter (its emulsion is already broken and will turn the dish greasy).
