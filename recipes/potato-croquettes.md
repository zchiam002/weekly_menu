# Potato Croquettes

> Italian potato croquettes (crocchè) — boiled potatoes mashed with egg yolk, Parmigiano and nutmeg, shaped into logs, breaded and fried until golden and crisp.

**Servings:** ~30 pieces

## Ingredients

- 1 kg red potatoes
- 30g egg yolks
- 100g Parmigiano Reggiano DOP, grated
- Nutmeg, to taste
- Fine salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste

### Breading

- 130g eggs (about 2 medium), beaten
- Breadcrumbs, as needed

### To fry

- Peanut oil, as needed

## Method

1. **Boil:** Boil the red potatoes whole (skin on) until fork-tender. Drain, peel while still warm, and mash or rice into a smooth, lump-free purée.
2. **Season:** While still warm, mix in the egg yolks, grated Parmigiano Reggiano, a grating of nutmeg, salt and black pepper. Combine into a firm, even dough. Let cool.
3. **Shape:** Take portions and roll into cylinders (logs) of equal size — about 30 pieces.
4. **Bread:** Dip each croquette in the beaten egg, then coat fully in breadcrumbs. For an extra-crisp shell, double-bread (egg then breadcrumbs again).
5. **Fry:** Heat peanut oil to ~170–180°C (340–355°F). Fry the croquettes in batches until deep golden and crisp all over. Don't crowd the pan.
6. **Drain & serve:** Drain on paper towels, season with a little salt, and serve hot.
