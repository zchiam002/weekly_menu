# Radish Pork Rib Soup with Dates 红枣萝卜排骨汤

> A mild, comforting traditional Chinese soup — pork ribs and daikon simmered with ginger, sweetened naturally with red dates and finished with goji berries. Lightly seasoned so the natural flavour of the ingredients comes through.

**Servings:** 4

## Ingredients

- ½ pound (≈225g) pork ribs, cut into bite-sized pieces (ask the butcher to cut the rack in half horizontally, then cut along the bone)
- 1 medium daikon (≈1 pound / 450g), cut into big chunks
- 3 slices ginger
- A small handful red dates (红枣), 6–8, optional pitted
- A handful goji berries (枸杞), added at the end
- 4 cups water
- ½ tsp Chinese (black) vinegar
- Salt, to taste
- A touch of white pepper, to taste
- 2 stalks green onion, chopped (to top)

## Method

1. Cover the pork ribs with cold water in a pot, bring to a boil over medium-high heat, cook a few minutes until the ribs change colour. Discard the water, rinse under running water, drain and set aside.
2. In a ceramic pot or Dutch oven, add the ribs, daikon, ginger, red dates and 4 cups water.
3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then turn to low and simmer ~35 minutes with the lid on (simmer the ribs longer first if you like them fall-apart tender).
4. Season with Chinese vinegar, salt and white pepper; stir to mix.
5. Add the goji berries, simmer another 5 minutes before removing from heat.
6. Top with fresh chopped green onion to serve.

### Notes
- Red dates (红枣) are added per the household preference for a naturally sweeter, blood-tonifying soup — added at step 2 so they soften and release sweetness.
- Goji berries go in only at the end so they don't turn mushy.
