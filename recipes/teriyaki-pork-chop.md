# Teriyaki Pork Chop (日式照烧猪排)

> Japanese restaurant-style teriyaki pork chops — tender pork collar marinated with oyster sauce, garlic and ginger juice, pan-fried and finished in a sweet-savoury mirin–soy onion sauce.

**Servings:** 5–6

## Ingredients

### Marinated pork

- 440g (15.5 oz) pork collar, cut into 2.5cm thick pieces with shallow cuts scored into each
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 50ml (1.8 fl oz) water
- A few dashes ground white pepper
- 3 cloves garlic, lightly smashed
- Ginger juice (squeezed from 10g grated young ginger)
- 1 tbsp cornflour

### Sauce

- ½ large red onion, sliced
- 100ml (3.5 fl oz) water
- 100ml (3.5 fl oz) mirin
- 50ml (1.8 fl oz) light soy sauce
- 2 tbsp sugar

### To garnish (optional)

- Toasted white sesame seeds
- A handful of finely sliced or curled spring onions

## Method

1. **Marinate:** Combine the pork with the oyster sauce, water, white pepper, smashed garlic and ginger juice. Mix in the cornflour last to coat. Marinate at least 30 min (longer is better).
2. **Sear the pork:** Heat a little oil in a pan over medium-high. Sear the pork chops until golden on both sides and just cooked through. Remove and set aside.
3. **Sauce:** In the same pan, soften the sliced red onion. Add the water, mirin, light soy sauce and sugar. Bring to a simmer and let it reduce slightly into a glossy sauce.
4. **Combine:** Return the pork to the pan and turn to coat in the sauce, letting it glaze and the flavours soak in. Spoon sauce over as it thickens.
5. **Serve:** Plate the pork, spoon over the onion sauce, and garnish with toasted sesame seeds and spring onions.
