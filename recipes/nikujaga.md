# Nikujaga

> Japanese meat and potato stew — thinly sliced beef, potatoes, carrot, onion and shirataki simmered in a sweet-savoury dashi, mirin and soy broth, finished with snow peas.

**Servings:** ~4

## Ingredients

- 1 onion (8.8 oz, 250g)
- 1 carrot (4.5 oz, 127g)
- 3 Yukon gold potatoes (1.2 lb, 546g)
- 1 package shirataki noodles (7 oz, 200g)
- ½ lb thinly sliced beef (chuck or rib eye; can use thinly sliced pork)
- 8 snow peas (1 oz, 28g; or green beans / green peas)
- 1 tbsp neutral-flavoured oil (vegetable, rice bran, canola, etc.)

### Seasonings

- 2 cups dashi
- 4 tbsp mirin
- 4 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp sake
- 1 tbsp sugar

## Method

1. **Prep:** Cut the onion into wedges, the carrot into rolling/bite-size chunks, and the potatoes into large chunks (soak potatoes briefly in water to remove excess starch, then drain). Parboil and cut the shirataki into manageable lengths. Cut the beef into bite-size pieces. Blanch the snow peas briefly and set aside.
2. **Sauté:** Heat the oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the beef and cook until it changes colour. Add the onion and sauté until translucent, then add the carrot and potatoes and stir to coat.
3. **Simmer:** Pour in the dashi and bring to a simmer. Skim off any scum/foam. Add the shirataki, then the sake, mirin, sugar and soy sauce.
4. **Cook:** Cover with a drop lid (otoshibuta) and simmer over medium-low heat until the potatoes are fork-tender, ~15–20 min, occasionally spooning broth over the top.
5. **Finish:** Turn off the heat and let it rest so the flavours soak in. Add the snow peas just before serving for colour. Serve warm.
