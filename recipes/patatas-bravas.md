# Patatas Bravas

> The authentic Madrid-style tapas — potatoes fried in extra virgin olive oil, served with a spicy smoked-paprika brava sauce. No tomato, no garlic, no aioli (that's the Barcelona version).

**Servings:** 4 | **Prep:** 10 minutes | **Cook:** 35 minutes

## Ingredients

### Brava sauce

- ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil
- 2 tsp sweet smoked paprika
- 1 tsp hot smoked paprika
- 1 tbsp corn starch
- 1 cup (237ml) vegetable broth
- ½ tsp sea salt
- ½ tsp white wine vinegar

### Potatoes

- 2 large Yukon gold potatoes
- ¾ cup extra virgin olive oil
- Pinch sea salt

## Method

1. **Prep potatoes:** Peel the potatoes, rinse under cold water and pat dry. Cut each into roughly bite-size squares (quarter, then cut each quarter into ~6–9 even pieces). Place in a large bowl of cold water and set aside.
2. **Bloom the paprika:** Heat ¼ cup olive oil in a frying pan over medium heat for ~3 min, then transfer the oil to a bowl. Add the sweet paprika, hot paprika and corn starch; whisk until well combined.
3. **Build the sauce:** Return the oil mixture to the pan over medium heat and whisk while slowly pouring in 1 cup vegetable broth — whisk continuously so it emulsifies. Once incorporated, season with the sea salt and white wine vinegar. Whisk to a creamy sauce, then turn off the heat and set aside.
4. **Fry the potatoes:** Drain and pat the potatoes completely dry. Heat ¾ cup olive oil in a sauté pan over medium-high for ~2 min, then add the potatoes and toss to coat each piece. Cook, stirring occasionally, until golden — about 20 min. Transfer to paper towels, season with sea salt, and add to a shallow bowl.
5. **Serve:** Reheat the brava sauce over low heat, whisking ~2 min to bring it back together. Pour generously over the potatoes and serve at once.
