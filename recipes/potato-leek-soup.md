# Potato Leek Soup

> Rustic potato and leek soup — softened leeks and potatoes simmered in stock with cured fatty meat for depth, finished with cream and fresh herbs. Puree it smooth or leave it chunky.

**Servings:** 4

## Ingredients

- 4 oz (113g) hard salami, bacon or other cured fatty meat (optional — can skip)
- 1.5 lb (680g) leeks
- 1.5 lb (680g) potatoes (any kind; waxy preferred)
- 1 × 32 oz (946mL) carton stock (chicken or any kind)
- 1 cup (237mL) cream
- Salt
- Pepper
- Fresh rosemary and thyme (or whatever herbs you have)

> Treat the quantities as approximate.

## Method

1. **Render the meat:** Cut the meat into big chunks, put in a cold soup pot, and turn on moderate heat. Let the fat melt out, then brown the meat in its own fat, stirring occasionally so it doesn't burn.
2. **Prep the leeks:** Trim the root end, halve lengthwise, and slice crosswise into thin strips — use the white and green parts, but not the dry fibrous tops. Wash well: swish the cut leeks in a big bowl of water, then fill with excess water and lift the floating leeks off the top so the grit sinks.
3. **Soften:** Add the leeks to the meat, stir, and let soften a few minutes.
4. **Potatoes:** Peel and cut the potatoes into chunks as big as you like. Add to the pot, stir, and cook a few minutes.
5. **Simmer:** Pour in just enough stock to cover the solids (may not be the whole carton). Cover and simmer until the potatoes are fork-tender, ~30 min.
6. **Cream:** Stir in the cream and simmer until it thickens slightly.
7. **Puree:** Puree fully, partially (preferred), or not at all. Thin with more stock if desired.
8. **Season:** Adjust with salt, pepper and herbs to taste.
