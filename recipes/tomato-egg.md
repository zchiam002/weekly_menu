# Tomato & Eggs 番茄炒蛋

> Daddy Lau's classic Cantonese tomato and eggs — bare-minimum ingredients, no garlic, no soy sauce, no oyster sauce. 15 minutes start to finish.

**Servings:** 4

## Ingredients

### Main
- 1.5 lb (~680g) tomato
- 4 eggs
- 5 slices ginger
- 1 tbsp cornstarch
- 4 tbsp water (for slurry)
- 2 tbsp oil
- 3 tbsp water (for cooking the tomatoes)

### Seasoning
- ½ tsp salt (for the eggs)
- ½ tsp salt (for the tomatoes)
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 2 tbsp ketchup

## Method

1. **Peel tomatoes (optional):** Score an X on each tomato, place in a bowl, pour boiling water over and rest ~2 minutes until peels curl. Pull peels off. Halve, remove the stem core, cut each half into 4 wedges, keeping seeds intact.
2. **Cut ginger:** Slice 5 thin pieces, then julienne into thin strips.
3. **Prepare eggs:** Crack into a large bowl. Add ½ tsp salt. Beat vigorously to get air in.
4. **Cornstarch slurry:** Mix 1 tbsp cornstarch + 4 tbsp water in a small bowl. Set aside.
5. **Cook eggs:** Heat pan on high. When hot but not smoking, add oil and swirl. Pour in eggs, push gently with spatula to scramble. When ~80% cooked (~35 seconds), transfer back to the bowl.
6. **Cook tomatoes:** Pan still hot — add a bit more oil, then ginger. Add tomato wedges, stir-fry ~30 seconds. Add 3 tbsp water. Season with ½ tsp salt and 2 tbsp brown sugar (more sugar if tomatoes are very sour). Cook ~2½ minutes (cover with a lid if struggling to keep a boil).
7. **Thicken:** Stir the slurry, then slowly pour in, mixing as you go. Stop when sauce reaches the consistency you like — may not need all of it.
8. **Finish:** Return eggs to the pan. Stir and cook ~50 seconds. Turn off heat. Stir in 2 tbsp ketchup. Taste, adjust, serve.
