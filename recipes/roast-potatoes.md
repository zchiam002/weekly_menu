# Crispy Roast Potatoes

> Ultra-crispy roast potatoes — large chunks parboiled in alkaline (baking soda) water to rough up the surfaces, tossed in garlic-herb-infused fat, and roasted until shatteringly crisp outside and fluffy within.

**Servings:** ~6–8 as a side

## Ingredients

- Kosher salt
- ½ tsp (4g) baking soda
- 4 lb (about 2kg) russet or Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into quarters, sixths, or eighths (large 2–3 inch chunks)
- 5 tbsp (60ml) extra-virgin olive oil, duck fat, or beef fat
- Small handful rosemary leaves, finely chopped
- 3 medium cloves garlic, minced
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Small handful fresh parsley leaves, minced

## Method

1. **Preheat:** Heat oven to 230°C (450°F), with a rack in the centre.
2. **Alkaline parboil:** Bring 2 quarts (2L) water to a boil. Add 2 tbsp kosher salt, the baking soda, and the potatoes. Return to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook until a knife meets little resistance, about 10 minutes.
3. **Infuse the fat:** Meanwhile, combine the oil/fat, rosemary, garlic and a few grinds of black pepper in a small saucepan. Heat over medium, stirring, until garlic just turns golden, about 3 minutes. Strain through a fine sieve into a bowl; reserve the solids separately.
4. **Roughen up:** Drain the potatoes and let them rest in the pot 30 seconds for moisture to evaporate. Transfer to a large bowl, add the infused oil and a little salt, and toss hard until a thick layer of starchy slurry coats every chunk and the exteriors are well roughed up.
5. **Roast:** Spread on a rimmed baking sheet in a single layer, spaced apart. Roast until the bottoms are golden and crisp, ~20 min. Flip the potatoes and continue roasting until deep golden brown and crisp all over, 30–40 min more, turning once or twice.
6. **Finish:** Transfer to a bowl. Add the reserved garlic-rosemary solids and the minced parsley, toss to coat, season with more salt and pepper, and serve immediately.
