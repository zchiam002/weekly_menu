# Nasu Dengaku (Miso Glazed Eggplant) 茄子田楽

> Grilled Japanese eggplant brushed with a separately-simmered sweet miso glaze and finished under the broiler. Custard-soft interior, glossy sweet-salty top.

**Servings:** 4

## Ingredients

### Eggplant
- 2 eggplants (preferably long Japanese / nasu, or long Chinese)
- Toasted white sesame seeds, to garnish
- Perilla (shiso) leaves, shredded, to garnish

### Miso glaze
- 2 tbsp yellow miso paste (awase)
- 2 tbsp sake
- ½ tbsp toasted sesame oil
- ½ tbsp light brown sugar (white granulated works)
- 1 tsp mirin

## Method

1. **Prep:** Preheat the grill or broiler on medium. Wash and dry the eggplants. Remove stems, halve lengthways, and score the cut face in a 1cm crosshatch ~5mm deep. (If using globe eggplant, cut into 2–3cm rounds and score one side.)
2. **First grill — flesh side:** Lay halves cut-side toward the heat. Grill 5 minutes.
3. **Make the glaze (parallel):** Combine miso, sake, sesame oil, sugar, and mirin in a small saucepan. Bring to a gentle boil over medium, stirring continuously. Drop to low and simmer a few minutes until a spatula leaves a clean track on the pan bottom that holds for half a second. Pull off heat.
4. **Flip and continue:** Flip eggplant skin-side toward heat. Grill another 5 minutes.
5. **Test doneness:** Flip again. Pierce the thickest part with a chopstick — must go through with no resistance. If still firm, keep grilling 2–3 more minutes. **Do not glaze early.**
6. **Glaze:** Brush a generous, even coat of miso glaze across the scored face. Work it into the crosshatch.
7. **Broil to caramelise:** Grill 2–3 minutes more. Stand at the oven — miso burns fast. Watch for matte → glossy → darker high points on the crosshatch, and the smell shifting from sweet-savoury to toasted. Pull immediately.
8. **Garnish:** Plate, rest 1 minute, sprinkle toasted sesame seeds and shredded shiso.

### Notes
- Taste the miso first — awase varies from 5–13% salt. If sharp on the fingertip, drop miso by 1 tsp and add the same to sugar.
- Cook the eggplant fully soft *before* glazing. The broiler step caramelises only — it does not finish the cook.
- Glaze a hot eggplant, not a cooled one — cooled flesh won't grip the paste.
