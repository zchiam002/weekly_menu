# Minestrone Soup

> Hearty Italian vegetable soup — mirepoix and tomato base loaded with kidney beans, green beans, zucchini and spinach, finished with small pasta, Parmesan and basil.

**Servings:** 8

## Ingredients

- 1½ tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 onions, chopped
- 2 cups celery, chopped
- 5 carrots, peeled and sliced
- 2 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth)
- 2 cups water
- 4 cups tomato sauce
- ½ cup dry red wine (optional)
- 1 can (14.5 oz) kidney beans, rinsed and drained
- 1½ cups fresh green beans, trimmed into 1-inch pieces
- 2 cups baby spinach
- 3 small zucchinis, quartered and sliced
- 1 tbsp fresh oregano, chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh basil, chopped (plus more for garnish)
- Salt and cracked black pepper, to taste
- ½ cup small pasta (e.g. baby seashells), cooked separately al dente
- Grated Parmesan, for serving
- Extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling

## Method

1. **Sauté the aromatics:** Heat olive oil in a large stockpot over medium. Add garlic to infuse the oil; once fragrant, add onions, celery and carrots (mirepoix). Cook until softening.
2. **Build the liquid base:** Deglaze with the red wine. Add broth, water and tomato sauce, stirring into a rich tomato broth. Bring to a boil.
3. **Simmer with vegetables & herbs:** Reduce heat to low. Stir in kidney beans, green beans, spinach, zucchini, oregano, basil, salt and pepper. Cover and simmer 30–40 minutes until vegetables are tender.
4. **Serve:** Cook the pasta separately al dente. Spoon pasta into bowls and ladle soup over (or stir into the pot if serving immediately). Finish with Parmesan, fresh basil and a drizzle of EVOO.
