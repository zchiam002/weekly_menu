# Shepherd's Pie

> Classic baked lamb mince with onion, carrot, celery and corn in a savoury gravy, topped with buttery mash, Pecorino and panko, baked golden and crisp.

**Servings:** 8

## Ingredients

### Filling
- 1.5kg (3.3 lbs) lamb mince
- 2 brown onions, finely diced
- 3 carrots, finely diced
- 2 sticks celery, finely diced
- 2 corn cobs, kernels removed
- 5 cloves garlic, finely diced
- Small bunch rosemary, finely chopped
- Small bunch thyme, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 12 dashes Worcestershire sauce
- 3 tbsp flour
- 1 litre (4 cups) chicken stock
- Salt to taste

### Mash topping
- 2kg (4.4 lbs) potatoes, suitable for mashing (e.g. Sebago)
- 100g (3.5 oz) butter
- 100ml (3.5 fl oz) milk
- Handful panko breadcrumbs
- 30g (1 oz) Pecorino cheese, grated

## Method

1. Heat a large heavy pot over medium-high with a drizzle of oil. Brown the lamb mince in thirds (pinch of salt each), removing each batch as it browns.
2. Return the pan to high heat. Sauté onions and carrots 2–3 min. Add garlic, rosemary, thyme and celery; stir. Add tomato paste, Worcestershire and flour; cook 2–3 min, stirring.
3. Return the lamb with the chicken stock. Season and simmer on medium-low 20–30 min. Stir corn kernels through in the last 2 minutes.
4. Meanwhile, boil peeled potatoes in cold salted water until fork-tender (25–30 min). Mash with butter and milk until smooth.
5. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Transfer the meat to an oven dish (~30×25cm). Top with mash, peaking it for crispy edges. Sprinkle Pecorino and panko.
6. Bake 35–40 min until golden and crisp on top.
