# Hong Kong Style Baked Pork Chop Rice 港式焗猪扒饭

> Cha-chaan-teng classic — pan-fried marinated pork chop over egg fried rice, blanketed in a tomato-based sauce and mozzarella, then baked until golden and bubbling.

**Servings:** 6

## Ingredients

### Marinated pork chop
- 4 pcs pork loin / pork chop (total 330g)
- 2 cloves garlic, lightly smashed
- Half a thumb of young ginger, lightly smashed
- 4 tbsp light soy sauce
- 3 tbsp Chinese white rice wine
- 3 tbsp cornflour

### Fried rice
- 8 eggs, beaten
- 6 cups cooked rice (from 3 cups uncooked)
- 2 tsp chicken stock powder
- 2 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- A few dashes white or black pepper

### Tomato-based sauce
- 1 big red onion, sliced
- 2 shallots, sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 3 fresh button mushrooms, thinly sliced
- 1 whole tomato, cut into wedges
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 cup water
- ½ tsp sugar
- ½ tsp salt
- Some cornstarch solution

### Others
- ¾ cup mozzarella cheese

## Method

1. Marinate the pork chops, then pan-fry until cooked through.
2. Make the egg fried rice and spread it in a baking dish; rest the pork chops on top.
3. Cook the tomato sauce and pour over the pork chops.
4. Top with mozzarella. Bake at 180°C for 10 min, then 200°C for 5 min (15 min total) until golden and bubbling.
