# Dry Laksa

> A dry, paste-coated version of laksa — thick vermicelli tossed in a deeply spiced rempah with coconut milk, prawns, fish cake, cockles, and laksa leaves.

**Servings:** 4–6

## Ingredients

### Rempah (spice paste)
- 250g shallots, peeled
- 6 cloves garlic
- 1 inch turmeric
- 1 tsp toasted belacan (shrimp paste)
- 1 stalk lemongrass, sliced
- 3 tbsp dried shrimps, soaked and drained
- 250g dried chilli paste
- 6 slices galangal

### Noodles & toppings
- 500g thick vermicelli
- 200g bean sprouts
- 2 tbsp cooking oil
- 200g coconut milk
- 6 pieces tau pok (tofu puffs), cut into cubes
- 500g prawns, deshelled and deveined
- 200g fish cake, sliced into strips
- 50g laksa leaves, finely chopped
- 500g cockles (with shell), blanched and deshelled
- Sugar and salt to taste

## Method

1. **Blend the rempah:** Combine all rempah ingredients in a food processor and blend until smooth.
2. **Blanch noodles and sprouts:** Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch thick vermicelli briefly, drain, and set aside. In the same water, blanch bean sprouts, drain, and set aside.
3. **Cook the rempah:** Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pan. Add the blended rempah and stir-fry until fragrant and slightly dry.
4. **Add coconut milk and tau pok:** Pour in coconut milk and mix into the rempah. Add cubed tau pok and stir.
5. **Cook prawns:** Add prawns and stir-fry 1–2 minutes until they start to turn pink.
6. **Combine with noodles:** Add the blanched vermicelli and mix evenly so noodles are coated in the paste.
7. **Season and add:** Season with sugar and salt to taste. Add bean sprouts and fish cake strips and mix.
8. **Finish:** Stir in chopped laksa leaves and deshelled cockles. Toss one final time and serve.
