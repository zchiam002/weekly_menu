# Bolognese

> Slow-cooked authentic Italian ragù — pork, beef and pancetta with a soffritto base, passata and tomatoes, simmered low for ~4 hours.

**Servings:** Not specified in source (large batch, ~6–8)

## Ingredients

- 500g (17.6 oz) pork
- 500g (17.6 oz) beef
- 100g (3.5 oz) pancetta
- 1 brown onion, chopped
- 2 celery sticks, chopped
- 3 carrots, chopped
- 1 bottle Italian passata
- 100g (3.5 oz) tomato paste
- 400g (14.1 oz) peeled tomatoes
- 1 glass red wine
- 1 mug full cream milk (optional)
- Extra virgin olive oil (EVOO)
- Salt and pepper

## Method

1. Blend pancetta in a food processor until finely chopped.
2. In a large pot over low-medium heat, add 2 tbsp EVOO and the blended pancetta; sweat gently, then add onion, celery and carrots. Cook the soffritto with the lid on ~10 minutes, stirring every 2 minutes to prevent burning.
3. After ~7 minutes, when tender and glossy, add a splash of red wine, cover again and finish. Drizzle a little more EVOO, then add the beef and pork, breaking up the meat well and mixing into the soffritto.
4. Once the meat turns grey, pour in the remaining wine and mix. Simmer 10 minutes, then season generously with salt and pepper. Stir in the tomato paste, then add the passata and peeled tomatoes.
5. Cover and cook on low ~3 hours, stirring every 30 minutes so nothing sticks.
6. Uncover, stir, and cook a further 1 hour. Adjust seasoning to taste, scraping the bottom as you stir. Keep the heat low.
